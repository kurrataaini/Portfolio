Fish Freshness Classification using ResNet50

This research focuses on identifying the freshness of tilapia fish using Convolutional Neural Network (CNN), specifically the ResNet50 architecture. Data collection involved gathering images of tilapia fish across four days, where Day 1 is considered fresh and Days 2, 3, and 4 are considered not fresh.

During the preprocessing stage, data augmentation was applied including rotation and brightness adjustments, resulting in 1600 images, which were split into training, validation, and testing datasets with a ratio of 70:20:10.

The ResNet50 model was enhanced with dropout layers. Several experiments were conducted by varying the dataset size, number of epochs, and augmentation techniques using TensorFlow's ImageDataGenerator. Hyperparameters such as learning rate and optimizer were also tuned.

The final results showed that the model achieved its best performance at 300 epochs with an accuracy of 0.99, precision 0.99, recall 0.99, and F1-score 0.99.
